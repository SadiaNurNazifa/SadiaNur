Currently I am a Lecturer in Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Scholars, where I teach and mentor students in programming, systems design, and competitive programming. My research work revolves around Artificial Intelligence, Human-Computer Interaction, and their intersection with real-world impact.

I completed my B.Sc. in Computer Science and Engineering at the Military Institute of Science and Technology (MIST). Previously, I led the Science Subteam of MIST Mongol Barota in international rover competitions such as the University Rover Challenge and the Anatolian Rover Challenge, where our team secured multiple top positions globally. I have also contributed to interdisciplinary projects in environmental technology, healthcare, and robotics, and my research has been published in venues including Scientific Reports, Lecture Notes in Networks and Systems, and IEEE ICCIT.

Beyond teaching and research, I enjoy baking brownies and cakes, and a bit of crafting.
